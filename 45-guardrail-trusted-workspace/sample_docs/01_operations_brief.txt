SUBJECT: Operation IRON GUARDRAIL 26-2 — Concept of Operations (Draft v0.3)

1. PURPOSE. This brief documents the concept of operations for Operation IRON GUARDRAIL 26-2, a 2/1 platoon-level live-fire rehearsal vicinity Camp Pendleton 09-14 June 2026. Coalition observers from Australia (AUS) and the United Kingdom (GBR) are invited under existing FVEY arrangements.

2. PARTICIPATING UNITS. Echo Company, 2nd Bn 1st Marines (-) (Reinforced) with attachments from 1st MAW. Coalition observers: 2 personnel from 3 RAR (AUS) and 2 personnel from 42 Commando (GBR). Notional opposing force is portrayed by 1st Bn 4th Marines acting as a near-peer mechanized infantry company.

3. FORCE PROTECTION POSTURE. FPCON BRAVO will be in effect for the duration of the rehearsal. The vulnerability assessment identifies the western motor pool gate and the unmarked range entry on Old Stage Road as the two highest-risk ingress points. Manning roster for the entry control points is at Annex C; rotation schedule is attached at Annex D and is not to be released outside the I MEF FP cell.

4. INTELLIGENCE SUPPORT. 1st Intelligence Battalion will provide a tailored open-source intel package built from previously-released exercise products. No collateral SECRET reporting is required for this rehearsal. The G-2 estimate for the OPFOR cell uses publicly-available platform data on the BMP-3 and T-72B3 for vehicle role-play.

5. LOGISTICS. Class I, III, and V supply will be drawn from MCLB Barstow against existing exercise allocations. CSSD-11 will operate the forward distribution point. There are no proprietary contractor rate cards or vendor cost build-ups in this brief; all support is organic to the MAGTF.

6. RELEASABILITY INTENT. The author requests this brief be marked at the lowest level consistent with operations security, with appropriate caveat to permit FVEY observer read-in. Final marking is at the discretion of the I MEF G-2 OCA-equivalent reviewer.
